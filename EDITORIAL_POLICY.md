# Editorial Policy

## Purpose

The Radical Libertarian publishes essays, podcast references, resources, tools, and archives around liberty, decentralization, privacy, money, speech, culture, political theater, and personal sovereignty.

## Editorial Standard

Every piece should do at least one of the following:

- Clarify an idea
- Challenge a lazy assumption
- Offer a practical action
- Preserve older content
- Route attention to owned channels
- Help readers build independence

## Accuracy

Do not publish factual claims that are specific, recent, legal, financial, medical, or high-stakes without verification.

When in doubt:

- Say it is opinion
- Cite the source
- Avoid claiming certainty
- Use cautious phrasing

## Humor

Humor is allowed. It is part of the brand.

Use humor against:

- bureaucracy
- lazy thinking
- institutional theater
- surveillance creep
- narrative conformity
- platform dependence

Do not use humor for:

- harassment
- protected-class attacks
- threats
- calls for violence
- personal targeting

## Disclaimers

Use clear disclaimers where appropriate.

Examples:

- This tool is satire and educational self-reflection.
- This page links to LP.org as an external resource.
- This is commentary, not professional advice.
