# Content Guide

## Editorial Promise

The Radical Libertarian should feel sharp, funny, principled, anti-bureaucratic, and useful.

Not beige.
Not institutional.
Not fake-edgy LinkedIn cosplay.
Not ten pounds of manifesto stuffed into a two-pound paragraph.

## Core Content Types

### Essays

Use for long-form arguments, rants, cultural critiques, money critiques, tech/privacy breakdowns, and philosophical pieces.

Recommended structure:

1. Hook
2. Thesis
3. What people get wrong
4. Why it matters
5. Examples
6. Practical exit strategy
7. Quote/pullout line
8. Related reading or podcast CTA

### Podcast Pages

Use for episode summaries, embedded players, transcript links, clips, and guest notes.

Recommended metadata:

- Episode number
- Title
- Summary
- Topics
- YouTube URL
- Spotify URL
- Transcript URL
- Status

### Resource Pages

Use for practical references, directories, glossaries, reading lists, tools, and external links.

For LP.org / Libertarian National references:

- Present as external resources.
- Do not imply official representation.
- Keep copy independent and clear.

## Style Rules

- Prefer short paragraphs.
- Use strong H2/H3 sections.
- Use vivid concrete examples.
- Avoid vague “raise awareness” language.
- Do not make unsupported claims.
- Do not publish threats, harassment, or calls for violence.
- Satire is fine. Sloppy legal liability cosplay is not.
