The State Is A Fiction

A philosophical grenade aimed at legitimacy, narrative, paperwork, and power.

The spell works because people clap.

The state is not fiction in the sense that its agents, buildings, courts, budgets, and consequences are imaginary. The consequences are painfully real. The fiction is the story that converts raw power into moral inevitability.

A border is a line until enough people agree to treat it as destiny. A tax is a demand until enough people call it civic virtue. A regulation is a command until enough people decorate it with public-interest language.

Legitimacy is narrative with enforcement.

Power wants to be obeyed. But stable power wants more than obedience. It wants emotional participation. It wants the governed to explain the system to themselves in flattering language.

That is why every institution needs myths. Not always lies. Myths. Simplified stories that make the machinery feel natural, moral, and permanent.

The adult move is not denial.

Pretending the state does not exist is not philosophy. It is a great way to collect penalties with extra steps. The useful move is to understand the difference between reality and legitimacy.

Reality tells you the machine has power. Legitimacy asks whether it deserves worship.

Build without kneeling.

  • Know the rules that affect you.
  • Reduce unnecessary exposure.
  • Build private capacity.
  • Develop local networks.
  • Preserve your speech and records.
  • Do not confuse compliance with consent.
The state is a fiction with a billing department.

Respect consequences. Reject worship.

That is the line. You do not have to pretend the machine is imaginary to refuse treating it as sacred.
