# Brand Voice

## Voice Summary

Sharp, satirical, liberty-forward, anti-bureaucratic, philosophical, funny, and practical.

The brand should feel like someone who has read political philosophy, seen institutional nonsense up close, and still has jokes loaded.

## Tone Pillars

### 1. Funny But Not Unserious

Use humor to cut through fog.

Good:

> The state is a fiction with a billing department.

Bad:

> lol government bad

### 2. Provocative But Not Sloppy

Challenge assumptions. Do not make lazy claims.

Good:

> If your rights require permission, they are privileges with better lighting.

Bad:

> Everyone who disagrees is evil.

### 3. Principled But Not Wooden

Use conviction without becoming a lecture hall chair.

Good:

> Exit is a strategy. Voice is a suggestion box.

Bad:

> Liberty is important because liberty is important.

### 4. Practical Over Performative

Give readers something to do.

Examples:

- Read this
- Build this
- Audit this
- Save this
- Share this
- Host this
- Stop depending on this

## Approved Flavor

- Brutalist
- Punk
- Philosophical
- Street-smart
- Anti-fluff
- Comical
- Sincere
- Suspicious of power
- Pro-builder

## Avoid

- Calls for violence
- Harassment
- Unsupported factual claims
- Fake legal advice
- Fake financial advice
- Fake official representation
- Dehumanizing language
- Lazy conspiracy sludge
- Boring party brochure voice

## Core Phrases

- Freedom is not supposed to be comfortable.
- The leash is always marketed as convenience.
- Exit is a strategy. Voice is a suggestion box.
- The state is a fiction with a billing department.
- Build parallel rails.
- Archive the signal.
- Share the grenade.
